Making molds for votives!

My brain broke a little thinking about making molds of molds and casting them. Making the aligmnment keys for the molds was particularly challenging. At some point I stopped making it harder than it needed to be and went with an existing #d file of the Rapa Nui Island, (aka Easter Island) statues, called Moai. I've always had an affinity for the figures and the mystery surrounding them. It also seems a fitting artifact for looking forward into the future during this strange time. With Covid19, climate collapse and massive inequality, our time has shown a distinct lack of vision of how we want the world to become. Or we have a vision and it sucks. Like the Rapa Nui, we are possibly in deinal of the future of our world. But here's some cool statues!

2 Part Molds Using Rhino

I leaned heavily on this Instructables tutorial on making a mold in Rhino. The Silohette command helped the work flow but it still took a basic knowledge of Rhino, particularly not making open poly surfaces!

The silcone test prints didn't have matching keys. So I printed a sleeve to hold them together. The walls were also too thin to hold silcone so I taped them up. I also printed a key plate to make another set of molds.
